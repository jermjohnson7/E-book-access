Getting Better Over Time

How to Level Up
Your Prompt Game

The prompt is just the input. The quality of what you feed it determines everything. Here's how to sharpen your inputs so the outputs actually move the needle.

Replace Every Variable — No Exceptions

The [BRACKETS] aren't decoration. They're the steering wheel. Replace them with your actual industry, revenue model, and audience. Lazy inputs produce outputs that belong to nobody.

Add Context in Layers

Start with the Prime Prompt. Then feed the output back in with more context — your previous results, constraints, competitors. AI compounds when you treat it like a conversation, not a vending machine.

Always Run the Refinement

The refinement prompt is the stress test. It's designed to break weak answers. If the first output feels too agreeable, the refinement will expose the cracks — and that's where the real insight lives.

Save Your Best Outputs

Build a personal prompt library in Notion or NotebookLM. Track what context produced the best results. Over time, you'll have a custom AI system tuned specifically to your business.

Pro Tips

How Most People
Will Use This Wrong

Don't be most people.

⚡

Use One Tool Consistently

Pick your AI model — ChatGPT-4o, Claude, Gemini — and stay with it for the entire book. Models have different tones and output styles. Switching mid-session creates inconsistency in your outputs. Master one, then cross-train.

🧠

Set the Persona First

Every prompt specifies an AI persona — "Act as a Cynical VC," "Act as a Market Research Analyst." This isn't optional flavor text. The persona reshapes the entire response. A strategist and a motivational coach will give you wildly different answers. Run the right persona every time.

📂

Build a Business Knowledge Base

The book recommends Notion or NotebookLM repeatedly — and for good reason. Your outputs need a home. Save your Identity Audit, your Problem Statement, your Brand Framework. They feed each other. A business without documentation is just a series of expensive experiments.

🔁

Re-Run Prompts as Your Business Evolves

These prompts aren't one-time exercises. Run Prompt 1 again in 6 months with your updated skill set and revenue history. The inputs change, the outputs sharpen. This book has a longer shelf life than most courses you've bought.

🚫

Don't Skip the Implementation Step

Every prompt ends with one concrete action. Most people will read it, nod, and move on. The ones who take the action — even the small one — are the ones who compound. The gap between knowledge and results is always execution.
